The freelance visa is a
residence permit
for self-employed immigrants.
With a freelance visa, you can…
Live anywhere in Germany
Start a business
or become a freelancer
Bring your family to Germany
13
Visit other
Schengen countries
without a visa
12
Become a
permanent resident
after 3 to 5 years
9
Become a citizen after 5 years
This guide explains how to apply for a freelance visa in Berlin or in your home country.
The
artist visa
and the
language teacher visa
are the same thing as the freelance visa.
7
The application process is the same.
Who needs a freelance visa?
If you are not a citizen of the
EU
,
EEA
or Switzerland, you need a
residence permit
to live in Germany. The freelance visa is a type of residence permit.
If you are a
permanent resident
, you don’t need a freelance visa. You can already be self-employed.
Who needs a residence permit?
Two types of freelance visa
There are two types of freelance visa, because there are two types of self-employed people:
Freelancers
(
Freiberufler
)
Artists, teachers, scientists, engineers, software developers and other professionals with special qualifications. If you sell professional services, you might be a freelancer. Delivery drivers, influencers, website operators and commercial photographers are not freelancers.
55
Tradespeople
(
Selbständiger
or
Gewerbetreibender
)
If you are not a
Freiberufler
, you are
Selbständig
. If you sell products or apps, or if you are a co-founder, you are usually
Selbständig
.
30
The difference is important, because they have
different requirements
. For example, tradespeople need German clients, but freelancers don’t. The
required documents
are also different.
Are you a Freiberufler or a Selbständiger?
Freelance visa requirements
To get a freelance visa, you need…
17
Enough income
In Berlin, your
net income
must be more than your rent + your
health insurance
+ €
563
per month.
48
If you support children or a spouse, you need a higher income.
67
See the
cost of living
in Berlin.
Self-employed work
You must be self-employed.
61
You can’t be an employee with social benefits.
Fake self-employment
is not allowed.
3
If you only have one client, you might not get a freelance visa.
Permission to do this job
You must have all the permits and special qualifications needed for the job.
11
For example, doctors, engineers and lawyers need special qualifications. –
Find the requirements for your profession
If you are over 45 years old, and you are not a citizen of the Dominican Republic, Indonesia, Japan, the Philippines or Sri Lanka, you must have…
51
A private retirement pension
Your pension must guarantee €
1,612.53
per month for at least 12 years after you turn 67 years old.
52
or
a public pension
You need a guaranteed public pension in Germany, Australia, Bosnia-Herzegovina, Chile, India, Israel, Japan, Canada, Kosovo, Morocco, North Macedonia, Montenegro, Serbia, South Korea, Tunisia, Turkey or the United States.
53
If you made 5 years of public pension payments in Germany, you have a guaranteed public pension.
or
€
232,204
in business assets
If you are
self-employed, but not a freelancer
, you also need…
A local economic interest
If you are
Selbständig
, you must have clients in Germany, or hire German employees.
60
If you only have clients in other countries, you won’t get the freelance visa. If you are a
Freiberufler
, you don’t need German clients.
You do not need to speak German
to get a freelance visa.
26
You do not need a degree
or relevant education, except
if your profession requires it
. For example, you need a law degree to be a lawyer, but you don’t need a degree to be a freelance photographer.
11
If you can’t get a freelance visa, there are
other residence permits
to start a business in Germany. For example, you might qualify for
permanent residence
.
Freelance visa alternatives
How to apply in Berlin
You can apply for a freelance visa before or after you
move to Germany
.
To apply
after moving to Germany
, you must…
Come to Germany
Apply for a freelance visa at your local
Ausländerbehörde
. If you don’t live in Berlin,
find your local
Ausländerbehörde
and follow their instructions.
Get your freelance visa
Start your business
You can apply in Germany if…
You are a citizen of Australia, Canada, Israel, Japan, New Zealand, South Korea, the United Kingdom, the United States, Andorra, Brazil, El Salvador, Honduras, Monaco or San Marino
8
or
you already have a German
residence permit
, and you want to change it or renew it
or
you have an
EU Permanent Residence
in another country
or
you have an
opportunity card
or
you have a
job seeker visa
4
or
you have a
National Visa
for self-employment that you must
convert to a freelance visa
If you can’t apply in Germany, you must
apply in your home country
.
Your current residence permit
might allow self-employment
. If not, you must
wait 8 weeks to 7 months
for your freelance visa before you start working.
34
It might be cheaper and safer if you
apply in your home country
, then come to Germany when you have permission to start your business.
If you need help
, an
immigration lawyer
or a
relocation consultant
can take care of everything. It costs €
500
to €
2,000
.
43
It’s a
tax-deductible
expense.
1. Come to Germany
Come to Germany
and prepare your freelance visa application.
If you have a
National Visa
or
residence permit
Your current visa or residence permit
might allow self-employment
.
If it expires
after you applied for the freelance visa, you don’t need to leave Germany. It stays valid until you get your freelance visa.
If you are a citizen of Australia, Canada, Israel, Japan, New Zealand, South Korea, the United Kingdom, the United States, Andorra, Brazil, El Salvador, Honduras, Monaco or San Marino
You can come to Germany without a visa. You can stay 90 days in Germany without a residence permit. You can look for clients, and apply for the freelance visa in Germany.
8
You can’t start working before you get your freelance visa. Some people do it anyway.
If you have an
opportunity card
You can come to Germany, then convert your opportunity card into a freelance visa. You can’t be self-employed with an opportunity card; you must wait for your freelance visa.
10
If you have a
Schengen visa
Schengen visas are for tourism, not immigration. Usually, if you enter Germany with a Schengen visa, you can’t apply for a freelance visa
in Germany
. Your application will be rejected. You must
apply in your home country
.
24
If these options don’t apply to you, you must
apply for the freelance visa in your home country
.
18
2. Prepare your application
Before you apply for the freelance visa…
Find a place to live
It’s really hard to find your first apartment. It’s much harder for freelancers. Start looking as soon as you can. Your only options might be a
WG
or a furnished apartment.
28
You must be allowed to
register your address
. Your landlord must give you a
tenancy confirmation
. If you can’t get a tenancy confirmation, you might not get a freelance visa.
1
Your rent must be affordable. In Berlin, your income must be more than your rent + your
health insurance
+ €
563
per month before taxes.
48
Your apartment must be big enough. You need 9 m² per adult, and 6 m² per child.
68
Get health insurance
You need valid
health insurance
.
Ask my insurance expert
to help you
choose the correct health insurance
. Don’t choose insurance yourself; it’s a really bad idea. Many applications get rejected because people have the wrong health insurance.
20
Prepare the
required documents
This takes a few weeks. Start as soon as you can.
If you bring your family to Germany, you can apply for their
family reunion visa
at the same time. You must prepare their documents too.
While you wait for an
Ausländerbehörde
appointment, you should also…
Register your address
(
Anmeldung
)
The
Anmeldung
is very important
. When you register your address, you get a
tax ID
and a
registration certificate
. If you don’t do your
Anmeldung
, they might reject your application.
64
Open a bank account
You need it to pay your rent, pay your application fee, and get paid by your clients. You should open a separate bank account for your business. You can choose a bank from any EU country.
3. Apply for the freelance visa
You must apply for the freelance visa online.
4
You do not need an
Ausländerbehörde
appointment.
Apply for the Freelance visa
It costs €
100
. It’s cheaper for Turkish citizens. You can pay with Visa, Mastercard or PayPal. You can’t pay with cash or
Girocard
.
38
The fee is
tax-deductible
, so keep the receipt.
If you have too many documents,
merge them
or
make them smaller
.
After you pay and send your application, you can download a PDF. This is your proof of application. Save it for later.
4. Wait
The
Ausländerbehörde
will process your application. It takes
8 weeks to 7 months
. You must wait for their response. There is no way to track your application. If you wait more than 3 months, you can
sue the
Ausländerbehörde
.
While you wait, your current
National Visa
or residence permit might expire. It’s extended until the
Ausländerbehörde
processes your application.
35
If your visa or residence permit expires
If your current permit allows self-employment
, you can start your business already. If not, you must wait for your freelance visa.
What happens while you wait
5. Go to your appointment
A few months later
, the
Ausländerbehörde
will email you an appointment date. If you get an appointment, you are almost guaranteed to get your freelance visa.
Follow the instructions in the email. You might need to send extra documents, or bring them to your appointment.
What happens during your appointment
If your freelance visa is approved, you will get a second appointment to pick up your plastic residence card. While you wait,
you can start freelancing
. You will get
a document
that confirms this.
19
6. Get your freelance visa
Go to your second
Ausländerbehörde
appointment and pick up your freelance visa. Your new freelance visa is valid for up to 3 years.
16
You might get a shorter freelance visa if they are not sure that your business will work.
23
If you had a residence permit for over 3 years, you might get an “unrestricted” residence permit. It allows any kind of employment or self-employment.
Check your new
Zusatzblatt
to see what you can do.

How to apply in your country
You can also apply for a freelance visa in your country, then move to Germany.
25
This is
cheaper and safer
, because you can start working as soon as you arrive in Germany.
33
You don’t need to wait in Germany without an income.
How to apply in your country
You can also get an
opportunity card
, move to Germany, then convert it into a freelance visa. You can’t be self-employed with an opportunity card; you must wait for your freelance visa.
10
Required documents
These are the required documents
in Berlin
.
17
If you apply elsewhere, they require different documents. Check the website of the
Ausländerbehörde
, embassy or consulate where you apply. –
Find your embassy or consulate
Your documents must convince the Ausländerbehörde
that your business will work, and that you will make enough money to support yourself.
When you send your application
, use PDF files or photos. Don’t use word documents or other file formats. If you have too many documents,
merge them
or
make them smaller
. You can also
mail your documents
, but it’s not better or faster.
When you go to your appointment
, bring the documents that they ask for in your appointment confirmation.
23
Put dates on all your documents. Print everything on A4 paper. Do not staple the pages together. Some case workers also ask for documents that you already sent. It’s safer if you bring everything you have, including documents that you already sent.
2
Required documents for everyone
Passport
Include a colour scan of your passport in your application. When you
go to your appointment
, bring your original passport. You get it back during the appointment; they do not keep it.
63
Application form
(
Antrag auf Erteilung eines Aufenthaltstitels
)
If this is your first residence permit application, fill this form. Download the form
here
or
on Berlin.de
.
40
Proof of health insurance
You need
valid health insurance
.
Ask my insurance expert
to help you
choose health insurance for your work visa
.
Health insurance card
Show both sides of the card.
or
proof of
public health insurance
Get a
Mitgliedsbescheinigung nach § 175 SGB V
from your health insurer or your
health insurance broker
.
or
proof of
private health insurance
Bring your insurance contract, a
Mitgliedsbescheinigung nach § 257 SGB V
, and bank statements that show health insurance payments. Get these documents from your health insurer or your health insurance broker.
6
or
proof of
expat health insurance
If this is your first
residence permit
, expat health insurance is usually accepted. If you renew your freelance visa, or change an existing residence permit, you usually need
public
or
private
health insurance.
Proof of housing
You need a
place to live
where you can
register your address
. If you apply in Berlin, your home address must be in Berlin.
Lease
Bring the original, not a copy. It must show the size of your apartment. Your apartment must be big enough (9 m² per adult, 6 m² per child).
68
You must make enough money to pay your rent. It can be a temporary lease, a
WG
, a sublet or a student residence.
39
and
tenancy confirmation
(
Wohnungsgeberbestätigung
)
Some temporary apartments and sublets don’t give you this document. You might not get a freelance visa without it.
1
Bring it even if you registered your address.
41
and
registration certificate
(
Anmeldebestätigung
)
You must
register your address
to get this document. If you don’t have it, your lease and
tenancy confirmation
are sometimes enough. At least bring proof that you have a
Bürgeramt
appointment
.
5
and
proof of rent payments
You must show what you really pay for housing. Print recent bank statements that show rent payments.
47
Bank statements
It shows how much savings you have.
62
In Berlin, €
3,000
to €
5,000
in savings is usually enough.
27
In other cities, you might need around €
10,000
.
21
You don’t need a
blocked account
. If you can, use a German bank.
54
If it’s in another currency, convert it to euros.
Translations
If your documents are not in German, bring translations. For certificates and official documents, bring
certified translations
. They
sometimes
accept untranslated documents, but don’t take the risk.
31
Get translations from
Red Tape Translation
or
Lingoking
.

Required documents for a
Freiberufler
Revenue forecast

Make a spreadsheet of your
expected
monthly business income and expenses. Use the

.
49
Your numbers must be realistic, but ambitious.
At least 2
letters of intent
(
Absichtserklärung
)
57
These are letters from potential clients that want to hire you. They are very important. They prove that you will find clients and make money. They must show what work you will do, and how much you will get paid. Signed contracts are even better.
If you are
Selbständig
, you need at least 2 letters from clients in Germany. If you are
Freiberufler
, you don’t need German clients.
60
If you are an artist or a language teacher, you can also bring…
Proof of financial support
50
If you can’t prove that you will make enough money, bring proof of financial support. For example…
A
blocked account
36
Your own savings
Regular bank transfers from your family
A letter from a guarantor (
Verpflichtungserklärung
)
32

Occupation practice permit
(
Berufsausübungserlaubnis
)
You must have all the permits and special qualifications needed for the job.

Required documents at your appointment
Appointment confirmation
You can’t enter the
Ausländerbehörde
without it. They check at the door. Have it on your phone or print it.
14
Passport
You get your passport back during your appointment; they do not keep it.
63
QR code for biometric pictures
(35 × 45 mm)
Printed passport photos are not allowed. Only QR codes are accepted. See
how to get biometric pictures
.
Cash, Visa, Mastercard or
Girocard
You must pay the €
100
application fee.
42
This fee is
tax-deductible
, so keep the receipt.
Optional documents
These documents are not required, but some
Ausländerbehörde
employees ask for them. They make your application more convincing.
Letters of recommendation
These are letters from your previous employers, clients or professors that recommend you for your work. If you are a scientist or researcher, this is very important.
44
The letters must be related to the work you do. Get 3 or 4 letters if you can.
29
Contracts
It proves that you have guaranteed work. Contracts are even better than
letters of intent
. The contract should explain what you will do, how long you will work and how much you will get paid.
Resume
or
CV
Work experience is not required, but it helps.
45
Your resume should prove that you have experience in your field. Don’t include irrelevant experience.
Cover letter
It explains what you do, and how your business works. It helps the
Ausländerbehörde
understand what you do. It helps you during your appointment, especially if you don’t speak German.
Portfolio
or
examples of your work
It explains what you do, and that you will find clients. If you are an artist, this is very important.
56
For example, you can bring photos of your work, posters of your events, screenshots of your website or apps or newspaper articles about you. You need printed examples; a website address does not work.
46
Proof or education
If your education is relevant to your business, prove it. Bring the original diplomas if you can. Copies also work.
37
If you have no relevant education, it’s not a problem.

Travel with a freelance visa
Your freelance visa lets you visit other
Schengen area
countries for up to 90 days without a visa. You can travel outside of Germany for up to 6 months.
22
If you travel longer, your freelance visa expires.
Your freelance visa lets you live anywhere in Germany, not just in the city where you applied.
Travel with a residence permit
Renew your freelance visa
Your freelance visa is valid for up to 3 years.
15
In Berlin, you must renew it around 6 weeks before it expires, not sooner.
How to renew a freelance visa
Apply for permanent residence
If you have a freelance visa, you qualify for
permanent residence
after 5 years. If you see “§ 21 Abs. 1” or “§ 21 Abs. 2a”
on your freelance visa
, you qualify after 3 years.
How to apply for permanent residence
If you plan to become a permanent resident, start making voluntary
public pension
payments. You usually need 60 months of pension payments to qualify for permanent residence. The minimum payment is €
111.97
per month. You can also get a private pension instead.
Pension requirements for permanent residence
